<objective>
Add one pure-core module `packages/core/src/dex/shared-progress.ts` that projects a locally-derived `DexStats` into a lean, serializable `SharedProgress` summary (PROG-01), reconstructs a minimal `DexStats` back from that summary for the UNCHANGED `compareDexes` (PROG-06/07), selects a friend's top-N rarest catches (PROG-08), and zod-validates an untrusted synced summary at the read boundary (D-19). This is where nearly all phase correctness lives — it is fully Node-unit-testable with zero mocks.

Purpose: The whole phase premise is "reuse, don't reinvent." The live head-to-head must reach the exact shipped `compareDexes` diff by feeding it a reconstructed `theirs`, with zero change to the diff logic and zero change to the file-import path. This plan builds and pins that reconstruction contract with the round-trip fidelity invariant.

Output: `shared-progress.ts` (projector + reconstruction + rarest selection + zod schema/parse), the `index.ts` exports, and `shared-progress.test.ts` (round-trip + unit tests). No app code, no Supabase, no DOM.
</objective>
