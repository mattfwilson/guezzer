<objective>
Replace the dex rarity model with tie-inclusive, play-count bands and add an "Epic" tier between Rare and Legendary. Every song's tier is now a pure function of its total corpus playCount:

| Tier | playCount | corpus count (verification target) |
|------|-----------|------------------------------------|
| legendary | exactly 1 | 32 |
| epic | 2–3 | 29 |
| rare | 4–8 | 32 |
| uncommon | 9–23 | 62 |
| common | 24+ | 109 |

This is a DECIDED change already modeled against the real 738-show corpus with the owner. Do not re-explore alternatives — implement exactly as specified. Direction is unchanged (fewest plays = rarest = legendary). The quantile ranking is replaced; the RARITY_MIN_PLAYS "fake legendary" cap is retired by design (accepted trade-off: curated + sentinel-filtered corpus).

Purpose: A played-once-ever song IS the ultimate catch. Tie-inclusive bands stop equally-rare songs from being split across tiers by arbitrary songId tie-breaking, and Epic gives the crowded low-play end a second, distinct tier.

Output: Core rarity logic + config rewritten; the RarityTier union widened to include `epic`, rippled cleanly through every consumer (caught at typecheck); tests rewritten for band semantics; the Epic label + hue added to both app TIER_COLOR maps.
</objective>
